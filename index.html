Who are Philemon and Minerva Morriss?

Philemon and Minerva Morriss crossed the Oregon Trail in 1852, leaving Iowa in April and settling in Sublimity, Marion County, Oregon in September. Philemon kept a diary of the trail detailing miles made, sites seen, and other details. This diary has generated significant interest in the couple and their history, especially among descendents, many of whom still live in the Pacific Northwest.

The couple met and married in Sangamon County, Illinois in April 1845 after Philemon lost his first wife, Nancy Jackson, early that year. Philemon had four living children from his first marriage:

  • Julia Ann, born 1833 in New Salem, IL, child of Philemon and Nancy Jackson
  • Survigny, born 1835 in New Salem, IL, child of Philemon and Nancy Jackson
  • Elizabeth, born 1838 in New Salem, IL, child of Philemon and Nancy Jackson
  • Douglas, born 1843 in New Salem, IL, child of Philemon and Nancy Jackson.
  • Philemon and Nancy had a number of other children -- Lucy Ann (twin to Julia Ann) and Thomas -- who died in 1840 as young children.

Philemon and Minerva stayed in Illinois for a few years after their marriage. During this time, Philemon fought in the Mexican American War and Minerva gave birth to Philemon Delacy and Amanda. The family soon moved to Polk County, Iowa. There, they had Annis, named for Minerva's mother and sister, before leaving on the trail in 1852:

  • Philemon Delacy, born 1846 in New Salem, IL, child of Philemon and Minerva Pierce
  • Amanda, born 1848 in New Salem, IL, child of Philemon and Minerva Pierce
  • Annis, born 1850 in Polk County, IA, child of Philemon and Minerva Pierce

Philemon and Minerva had four more children once they got to Oregon:

  • Albert, born in 1853 in Sublimity, Oregon, child of Philemon and Minerva Pierce
  • Narcissa, born 1855 in Sublimity, Oregon, child of Philemon and Minerva Pierce
  • Cordelia, born 185_ in Sublimity, Oregon, child of Philemon and Minerva Pierce
  • Edwin, born 185_ in Sublimity, Oregon, child of Philemon and Minerva Pierce

Get Official Documents and Family Remembrances Here

Many descendents have lovingly crafted histories and done research on the Morriss family. Many have connected on Ancestry.com and by phone and email. This website exists to consolidate all records on Philemon and Minerva in one place. You can simply download a .zip file of documents. On this site, you'll find:

  • The scanned diary, in its entirety, from Gary Barker of Salem, OR
  • Original survey and land records
  • Court documents
  • Marriage certificates
  • ...and more.
